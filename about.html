Generally speaking, a Supply Chain Analyst is responsible for increasing the efficiency of shipping procedures. Although stock rooms for large businesses can get hectic and overcrowded, an analyst will ensure that everything runs smoothly.

Accurately assessing and acting on supply chain data starts with visibility. Another important goal of supply chain analytics is to improve forecasting and efficiency, to be more responsive to customer needs. Predictive analytics can help anticipate consumer demand, which you can use to identify opportunities to save costs, adjust inventory, and accelerate delivery throughout the supply chain.

I am a Data Analyst with a focus on supply chains. The goal of my projects and blog posts is to develop new insights and systems for analyzing supply chains of all kinds, ultimately resulting in more profitable decisions for business managers which will have the greatest possible impact on the bottom line.
