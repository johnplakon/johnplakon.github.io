Analyzing Hotel Reservation Cancellations

Unveiling Insights with SQL

Posted by John Plakon on March 3, 2021

Introduction

The advent of online hotel reservation channels has revolutionized the way customers book their accommodations. However, this convenience also brings about challenges for hotels, with cancellations and no-shows becoming prevalent issues. In this blog post, we'll dive into a dataset from Kaggle, specifically the "Hotel Reservations Classification Dataset," to explore the factors behind hotel reservation cancellations and gain valuable insights. Leveraging SQL code, we'll analyze the dataset and uncover intriguing trends and patterns.

Dataset Overview

The dataset provides us with information on hotel reservations, including various attributes that can contribute to cancellations or no-shows. Before we proceed with the analysis, let's familiarize ourselves with the dataset's structure:

Column Description
reservation_id Unique identifier for each reservation
hotel_id Identifier for the hotel
customer_id Identifier for the customer
booking_date Date when the reservation was made
checkin_date Date when the customer is expected to check-in
checkout_date Date when the customer is expected to check-out
reservation_status The status of the reservation (e.g., canceled, checked-in, checked-out)
cancellation_reason Reason for cancellation, if applicable
room_type Type of room reserved
booking_channel The channel through which the booking was made (e.g., website, phone, travel agent)
total_cost Total cost of the reservation

Analyzing Cancellation Reasons

To understand the primary reasons behind reservation cancellations, we can utilize the cancellation_reason column. Let's query the dataset to identify the top cancellation reasons: 


SELECT cancellation_reason, COUNT(*) AS cancellation_count
FROM reservations
WHERE reservation_status = 'Canceled' AND cancellation_reason IS NOT NULL
GROUP BY cancellation_reason
ORDER BY cancellation_count DESC;

The SQL code above retrieves the cancellation reason along with the count of occurrences, filtering only canceled reservations with a specified cancellation reason. This query will help us identify the most common causes for cancellations.

Analyzing Booking Channels

Next, let's explore the booking channels that customers prefer when making their reservations. This analysis can provide insights into the effectiveness of different channels and potentially guide hotels in optimizing their marketing strategies: 


SELECT booking_channel, COUNT(*) AS booking_count
FROM reservations
GROUP BY booking_channel
ORDER BY booking_count DESC;

By executing the above SQL query, we obtain the count of reservations made through each booking channel, allowing us to identify the most popular and influential platforms.

Identifying Peak Cancellation Periods

Understanding the temporal distribution of cancellations can help hotels prepare for peak periods and manage their resources effectively. Let's examine the cancellation patterns across different months: 


SELECT EXTRACT(MONTH FROM booking_date) AS month, COUNT(*) AS cancellation_count
FROM reservations
WHERE reservation_status = 'Canceled'
GROUP BY month
ORDER BY month;

The SQL code above retrieves the month from the booking_date column and counts the number of cancellations for each month. Sorting the results by month will provide a clear overview of cancellation trends over time.

Conclusion

By leveraging the power of SQL and analyzing the "Hotel Reservations Classification Dataset," we have gained valuable insights into reservation cancellations. Through analyzing cancellation reasons, booking channels, and cancellation patterns over time, hotels can enhance their decision-making processes, optimize their operations, and potentially reduce revenue loss due to cancellations. With the knowledge obtained from this analysis, hotels can better understand their customers' behavior and adapt their strategies accordingly, fostering improved customer satisfaction and revenue generation.
